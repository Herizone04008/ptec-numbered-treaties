Treaty 1 Territory and Land Acknowledgment

Treaty 1 Territory

Treaty 1 Territory is home to the Anishinaabe, Cree, Oji‑Cree, Dakota, and Dene Peoples, and is the homeland of the Métis Nation. Understanding the land we live on helps us recognize our responsibilities, our shared history, and the ongoing relationships between Indigenous and non‑Indigenous peoples.

Where Is Treaty 1?

Treaty 1 covers much of southern Manitoba, including Winnipeg and surrounding communities. It was the first of the numbered treaties signed in Western Canada, marking a major moment in the relationship between Indigenous Nations and the Crown.

The treaty outlines agreements about land sharing, responsibilities, and ongoing commitments that continue to shape life in Manitoba today.

Why Land Acknowledgments Matter

A land acknowledgment is more than a statement — it is a reminder of the history, culture, and presence of Indigenous Peoples. It encourages respect, awareness, and responsibility.

When we acknowledge Treaty 1 Territory, we recognize the original caretakers of this land and commit to learning, understanding, and supporting reconciliation.
