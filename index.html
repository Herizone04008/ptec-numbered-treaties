Treaty 1 – The Stone Fort Treaty

Where the Treaty Was Negotiated

Treaty 1 was negotiated in 1871 at Lower Fort Garry in Manitoba. This historic location was an important meeting place during the fur trade era. Government representatives and leaders from the Anishinaabe and Swampy Cree Nations met there to negotiate an agreement about land and relationships between Indigenous peoples and the Crown.

Treaty 1 Territory

Treaty 1 territory is located in southern Manitoba. The land includes the region around present-day Winnipeg and surrounding communities. Maps of the treaty area help show where the land is located within the province and how it connects to other treaty territories.

First Nations Communities

Several First Nations signed Treaty 1 in 1871. These communities belong mainly to the Anishinaabe (Ojibwe) and Swampy Cree Nations. Some of the communities include Brokenhead Ojibway Nation, Sagkeeng First Nation, and Long Plain First Nation.
